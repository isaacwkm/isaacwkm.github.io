Hi, I am Isaac Kim, a game programmer and technical collaborator interested in building expressive, systems-driven interactive experiences. My work often sits between gameplay engineering, simulation design, tooling, and team leadership, with a focus on making game systems feel clear, testable, and satisfying to play.

I enjoy working in Unity and C#, and I like projects where design and engineering have to meet in the middle. I am profecient at implementing gameplay systems end-to-end, refactoring systems as they grow larger, and performing debug and QA tests. I care about how other department's work ties into mine, and strive to be an addition to a team that makes everyone's job easier.

This site collects selected projects, experiments, and development notes from my game development work.
